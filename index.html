how i got here

i started inside the united nations at sixteen — beijing 25+ task force, generation equality, csw. i learned how institutions breathe, how policy moves from language to lives, and that being the youngest person in the room is a superpower if you use it right.

at barnard i studied econ and data science, graduated a semester early, and founded the college's first women-focused investment club because the recruiting process made it painfully clear no one was going to build it for us.

now i'm in finance. not because the un work was a phase — because i wanted to learn the side of capital that the rooms i grew up in were always reacting to.
